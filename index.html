01 / The Goal

What is Zero Hunger?

The UN's Zero Hunger initiative aims to end hunger, achieve food security, improve nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture by 2030. It focuses on ensuring that everyone has reliable access to sufficient, safe, and nutritious food — while building more resilient and equitable food systems worldwide.

"While global hunger has declined, progress is uneven — and certain regions still face severe food insecurity."

02 / Hunger Trend

Global Undernourishment, 2000–2024

% of world population undernourished · Source: Our World in Data

2000 Baseline

12.7% of the global population — nearly 800 million people — were undernourished. Progress was already underway, driven by economic growth and improved agricultural productivity.

2020 — COVID Disruption

The COVID-19 pandemic disrupted food systems globally, reversing years of progress. Hunger rose to 8.8%. Supply chain failures, income shocks, and conflict compounded the setback.

2024 — Progress Stalls

By 2024, the rate sits at 8.2% — only a modest improvement from the pandemic peak. The decline has flattened. At this pace, the 2030 Zero Hunger target remains out of reach.

Chart 1 Takeaway

From 2000 to 2024, the global share of undernourished people fell from 12.7% to 8.2% — real progress over two decades. But the trend is not smooth. Around 2020, the COVID-19 pandemic pushed the rate back up to 8.8%, and improvement has slowed since. The long-term direction is positive, but the recovery is fragile.

Source: Our World in Data, "Share of people who are undernourished"

03 / Food Waste

Food Waste Per Capita by Region, 2022

Food waste per capita in 2022, by SDG region, split across retail, out-of-home consumption, and household. Sub-Saharan Africa tops the list — driven by limited refrigeration, long and fragile supply chains, and a hot climate that accelerates spoilage. The same region carries some of the highest per-capita hunger rates. Investment in cold-chain and transport infrastructure may be the most direct route from food grown to food eaten.

Source: Our World in Data, "Food Waste Per Capita, 2022" (FAO + UNEP)

04 / Child Stunting

Child Stunting Rates by Region

Child stunting rates — long-term malnutrition that shapes physical growth, brain development, and lifetime health — are starkly unequal across regions. In Southern Asia and Sub-Saharan Africa, nearly 1 in 3 children are affected. In Europe and North America, rates are an order of magnitude lower. Global averages hide this divide. Targeted intervention in the most-affected regions matters more than assuming progress is universal.

Source: UNICEF, 2023 Joint Child Malnutrition Estimates

05 / Double Burden

Proportion of Overweight Children Under 5

[ Image: assets/un-overweight-under5.png ] Drop PNG into /assets/ to display

The share of children under 5 who are overweight has stagnated near 5.5% since 2012, well short of the 2030 target of 3%. Malnutrition is not only a story of scarcity.

Source: UN SDG Report 2023, Goal 2

Overweight and obesity — driven by access to cheap, calorie-dense food — coexist with undernutrition. This "double burden" means that food systems must simultaneously address scarcity and surplus, requiring policy responses that go beyond simply increasing supply.

06 / Price Volatility

Countries Experiencing High Food Prices

[ Image: assets/un-food-prices.png ] Drop PNG into /assets/ to display

The share of countries experiencing moderately to abnormally high food prices dropped from 48.1% in 2020 to 21.5% in 2021 — still elevated versus pre-pandemic norms.

Source: UN SDG Report 2023, Goal 2

Supply chain disruption, energy and transport costs, and political instability keep food prices volatile. High prices hit the poorest households hardest — the same households where undernourishment rates are already elevated.
